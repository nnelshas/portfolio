Resume (PDF)

I’m a Robotics PhD candidate at Georgia Tech (NSF-GRFP Fellow 2024, expected May 2027) researching real-time sensing and control for highly dynamic, uncertain physical systems. My PhD thesis — “Multi-material Mass Flow Monitoring and Control for Powder-Blown Directed Energy Deposition” — combines multi-physics sensor fusion, provably-stable adaptive control, and machine learning for next-generation metal additive manufacturing. In parallel, I collaborate with Los Alamos National Laboratory on perception and inspection algorithms for autonomous NDE.

Research Highlights

Adaptive Control

MRAC with Lyapunov-stable parameter adaptation for feedforward control of nonlinear systems with large delays. Derived from first principles — stability guaranteed.

Multi-Physics Sensing

Designed 3 novel in-line sensors (piezoelectric, triboelectric, optical) with Bayesian fusion for robust state estimation under noise and disturbances.

Learning-Based Systems

VLA foundation model UQ (99.2% on LIBERO), deep RL for nonlinear control, PLSR multi-material sensing, and sensor distillation via cross-modal knowledge transfer.

Selected Work

  • MRAC + Lyapunov Adaptive Control (Stebner Lab): Derived Lyapunov-stable adaptation laws for Model Reference Adaptive Control of powder transport with 10–60s delays; 70% fluctuation mitigation demonstrated. FPGA-deployed.

  • Multi-Physics Sensor Array (Stebner Lab): Designed and validated 3 novel in-line sensors (piezoelectric, triboelectric, optical) with Bayesian fusion for real-time mass flow monitoring in PB-DED. 5 patents pending (GT) · 1 patent pending (LANL).

  • Deformable Convolution for Curved-Surface NDE (LANL): Invented deformable kernel wavenumber filtering for accurate full-field thickness estimation on arbitrary curved geometries. Patent pending, targeting MSSP 2026.

  • Ultrasonic Inspection UQ (LANL): Theoretical UQ framework propagating measurement uncertainty through full inspection pipelines. Published at IWSHM 2023, presented at AIVELA 2025.

  • Vision-Language Action Models (CS 8803): Bayesian LoRA + Laplace approximation for OOD uncertainty quantification on OpenVLA; 99.2% success on LIBERO via confidence-based action allocation with ~2× inference speedup.

selected publications

  1. Real-time powder flow monitoring for directed energy deposition using multi-physics sensing
    Neel Shah, Z. Brunson, A. Panchal, and 2 more authors
    2026
    Manuscript in preparation, targeting Additive Manufacturing, April 2026. 3 patents pending (Georgia Tech).
    Abs Bib

    We present a multi-physics sensor array comprising four orthogonal in-line sensing modalities — low-frequency piezoelectric, triboelectric, optical attenuation, and optical reflection — for real-time mass flow monitoring in powder-blown directed energy deposition (PB-DED). Each modality captures distinct physical properties of the powder stream. A Bayesian sensor fusion pipeline combining Savitzky-Golay filtering, least-squares oscillation fitting, and SNR quantification provides robust average flow rate estimates and high-fidelity detection of RPM-tied periodic instabilities. Validated against precision mass scale ground truth across 2–7 RPM.

  2. Real-time adaptive feedforward powder flow control for directed energy deposition
    Neel Shah, Z. Brunson, and A. Stebner
    2026
    Manuscript in preparation, targeting Additive Manufacturing, November 2026. Patent in preparation (Georgia Tech).
    Abs Bib

    We develop a feedforward mass flow controller for powder-blown DED combining physics-based system identification, analytical control law inversion, and Model Reference Adaptive Control (MRAC) with Lyapunov-stable parameter adaptation. The system identification characterizes rotary-driven powder transport dynamics as a function of motor RPM, identifying oscillation frequency and amplitude parameters via least-squares regression. A feedforward control law is derived by analytical inversion of the identified model. MRAC with Lyapunov-derived adaptation laws updates model parameters in real-time using delayed process sensor feedback, handling transport delays of 10–60 seconds. Preliminary implementation demonstrates 70% mass flow fluctuation mitigation.

  3. General Curved Surface Full-field Thickness Estimation utilizing Deformable Kernels for Wavenumber Filtering
    Neel Shah and others
    2026
    Manuscript in preparation, targeting Mechanical Systems and Signal Processing, April 2026. Patent pending (Los Alamos National Laboratory).
    Abs Bib

    We introduce a deformable convolution framework for wavenumber-domain filtering on arbitrary curved surfaces, enabling accurate full-field ultrasonic thickness estimation without the flat-geometry assumptions of standard methods. Kernel offsets are derived from surface principal curvatures obtained via 3D point cloud analysis, allowing the filter bank to adapt to local propagation geometry. This eliminates systematic bias artifacts and significantly improves anomaly localization accuracy on curved structural components.

  4. Uncertainty Quantification of Thickness Estimation via Full-field Ultrasonic Inspection
    Neel Shah, E. Jacobson, E. Flynn, and 1 more author
    In International Workshop for Structural Health Monitoring (IWSHM), 2023
    Also presented at AIVELA 2025
    Abs Bib

    We develop a rigorous theoretical uncertainty quantification (UQ) framework for full-field ultrasonic thickness estimation pipelines. By propagating measurement noise through each processing stage — from raw waveform acquisition through wavenumber filtering to final thickness reconstruction — we derive calibrated confidence intervals. Dominant error sources are identified, the amplification of uncertainty at high-curvature regions is characterized analytically, and probabilistic anomaly detection thresholds with well-calibrated false-alarm rates are provided.
